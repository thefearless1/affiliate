Top 5 Productivity Apps for Mobile in 2026

Ranked by how much they actually improve your day — not by feature count or App Store ratings.

Last updated: April 5, 2026

1
Notion Best Overall

Notion has matured into the most flexible personal productivity platform available. Notes, databases, project boards, wikis, and AI writing assistance all in one app. The mobile client is fast and capable — you can do real work from your phone, not just review tasks.

Pros
  • Extremely flexible structure
  • Great AI assistant built in
  • Solid offline support
  • Generous free tier
Cons
  • Can be overwhelming to set up
  • AI features cost extra
  • Slow on older phones
Free · Plus from $10/mo
Try Notion →
2
Todoist Best Task Manager

If you want to manage tasks and nothing else, Todoist is the benchmark. Natural language input, smart scheduling, recurring tasks, and karma streaks keep you consistent. Polished on every platform, with one of the best widgets on both iOS and Android.

Pros
  • Best-in-class natural language input
  • Excellent widgets on iOS & Android
  • Reliable sync across devices
  • Clean, distraction-free UI
Cons
  • Note-taking is basic
  • Pro tier needed for reminders
  • No calendar view on free plan
Free · Pro from $4/mo
Try Todoist →
3
Obsidian Best for Note-Takers

Obsidian stores your notes as plain Markdown files on your device — no vendor lock-in, no proprietary format. Its bidirectional linking and graph view help you build a connected knowledge base over time. The mobile app is fully featured and syncs via iCloud, Dropbox, or Obsidian Sync.

Pros
  • Plain text files — yours forever
  • Powerful plugin ecosystem
  • Works fully offline
  • Free for personal use
Cons
  • Sync costs extra ($4/mo)
  • Setup takes time and effort
  • Not beginner-friendly
Free · Sync from $4/mo
Try Obsidian →
4
Superwhisper Best Voice-to-Text

Superwhisper runs OpenAI's Whisper model on-device for fast, private, and accurate voice transcription. Dictate notes, compose messages, and draft emails anywhere — it types for you. A genuine productivity multiplier for anyone who thinks faster than they type.

Pros
  • On-device — fully private
  • Works offline
  • Extremely accurate transcription
  • Types into any app instantly
Cons
  • iOS / macOS only
  • Pro models require subscription
  • Niche use case
Free · Pro from $8.99/mo
Try Superwhisper →
5
Structured Best Daily Planner

Structured is a visual daily planner that puts your tasks on a timeline. Drag tasks into time slots, see your day at a glance, and focus on one thing at a time. Its clean design and Apple Watch complication make it perfect for people who think visually about their schedule.

Pros
  • Visual timeline-based planning
  • Beautiful, minimal UI
  • Apple Watch support
  • Calendar integration
Cons
  • iOS / macOS only
  • No Android version
  • Limited project management
Free · Pro from $3.99/mo
Try Structured →
