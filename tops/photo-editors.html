Top 5 Photo Editors in 2026

Tested across RAW processing, retouching, and everyday use — these are the tools worth your money.

Last updated: April 10, 2026

1
Adobe Lightroom Best Overall

Lightroom remains the gold standard for photographers who shoot in RAW. Its non-destructive workflow, excellent masking tools, and tight integration with Photoshop make it the most complete editing ecosystem available. The cloud sync across desktop and mobile is genuinely seamless.

  • Best-in-class RAW processing
  • Excellent AI masking & sky replacement
  • Cloud sync across all devices
  • Huge community & preset ecosystem
From $9.99/mo
Try Lightroom →
2
Capture One Pro Pick

Favored by commercial photographers, Capture One delivers exceptional color grading and tethered shooting support. Its layer-based editing and styles system gives more granular control than Lightroom. Steep learning curve, but unmatched output quality.

  • Superior color science
  • Best tethered shooting support
  • Powerful layer-based editing
  • One-time purchase available
From $16/mo or $299 one-time
Try Capture One →
3
Luminar Neo Most Fun to Use

Luminar Neo wins on AI-powered features. Sky replacement, portrait retouching, and relighting work impressively well with minimal effort. Great for photographers who want stunning results quickly without mastering complex tools.

  • AI tools that actually work
  • Fast, intuitive workflow
  • No subscription required (perpetual)
  • Great for beginners
From $9.95/mo or one-time
Try Luminar Neo →
4
Affinity Photo 2 Best Value

A one-time purchase that punches well above its price. Affinity Photo 2 handles RAW editing, compositing, and retouching with professional-grade tools. The UI mirrors Photoshop closely enough that switching is painless. No subscription, ever.

  • One-time purchase, no sub
  • Full RAW + compositing support
  • Fast and lightweight
  • Cross-platform (Win/Mac/iPad)
$69.99 one-time
Get Affinity Photo →
5
GIMP 100% Free

GIMP is the most capable free photo editor available. It handles everything from basic crops to advanced retouching and scripting. The interface takes getting used to, but it's hard to argue with free and fully open-source.

  • Completely free & open-source
  • Highly extensible via plugins
  • Available on Win/Mac/Linux
  • Powerful scripting support
Free — open source
Download GIMP →
